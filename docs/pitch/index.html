The Problem

Daily puzzle games on Reddit fear their own comment section.

  • One spoiler ruins the day → comments are treated as a hazard to be hidden.
  • Every player gets the identical puzzle → no visitor is ever needed; everyone is interchangeable.
  • Nothing on the platform makes a new reader structurally valuable to the crowd.
The Solution

REDACTED deals every viewer a different censored dossier.

  • Your handful of unredacted lines are shards the crowd doesn't have.
  • The case cannot be solved alone → the comment thread becomes the evidence board.
  • Spoilers are structurally impossible — nobody holds the whole truth.
  • Comment-reading becomes the core skill, not a hazard.
  • Every new reader — including every judge — adds information to the system.

Why This Can Only Be Reddit

The evidence board is a view over a threaded, identity-bearing comment section physically attached to the game state.

  • asUser SUBMIT_COMMENT — your evidence card posts under your username.
  • onCommentCreate trigger reconciles thread ↔ board.
  • Server-held truth + scheduler lifecycle (case-drop / drip / verdict crons).
  • Flair ladder (Beat Cop → Detective → Inspector), stickied verdict comments.

Remove Reddit and there is no port target — the thread is the game.

The Anti-Slop Moat

Our content pipeline is a linter, not a language model.

  • Zero AI-generated content anywhere. All 3 launch cases are handcrafted.
  • Every non-culprit eliminable by ≥2 shard-disjoint combinations.
  • A Monte-Carlo of 1,000 random 60% deals — all reach the truth.
  • Run with the same deduction engine as the runtime — play and lint can never diverge.
  • Not trivia. Not collaborative storytelling — one authored ground truth, a win/lose verdict.
The Population-Elasticity Valve

How a lone judge — or a 30-member sub — still works.

  • Reserved pivots: every first-seen account is dealt a board-absent shard.
  • Reserve-aware demo seed: Case #17 leaves the crowd-favorite standing on purpose — proven at build time (lint level LD).
  • Hourly drip: after hour 12 the highest-information unfiled shard is released publicly.
  • Cold Case Archive: closed cases replay solo against their solve timeline — no liveness dependency.
